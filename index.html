About

Developing flexibility, strength, and technique in athletes and performers, from gymnasts, dancers, figure skaters, contortionists, and aerialists to your very own little aspiring star. Building a classical and athletic foundation together, for life.

Instructor

Vita Bachman is originally from Kiev, Ukraine. She competed in rhythmic gymnastics nationally and internationally for eight years and reached the Candidate to Master of Sports level. She was trained by Natalia Kiselyova, a well-respected coach who worked with the Ukrainian National team. In 1996, Vita decided to pursue a degree in Business Administration from the International Christian University in Kiev. During her college years, she continued to coach rhythmic gymnastics under the close supervision and mentorship of her coach Natalia, who passed on her training and experience.

After moving to the United States in 2000 to pursue her MBA from the University of Tennessee, Vita continued to be involved in rhythmic gymnastics as a coach and a Junior Olympic judge in Nashville, Tennessee where she produced more than 25 state, regional, and national champions. Many of her students qualified for the Junior Olympics National Championships over the years. In 2005, she was elected the USA Gymnastics' Rhythmic Regional Chair, and served in this capacity for seven years. Vita’s gymnasts have always been known for having the classical, authentic, Eastern-European style training, clean execution and excellent technique.

In 2011, Vita’s husband’s job brought her to Dallas where she currently offers flexibility and strength training to dancers and athletes of various disciplines.

Vita offers private lessons at her home studio in Allen, TX as well as at several other locations in the Dallas area. She works with dancers from several local dance schools including the Chamberlain School of Ballet, The Dallas Conservatory, The Dance Space, and Booker T.W. Vita has worked with the Dallas Figure Skating Club athletes since 2015 and has been on staff with the Lone Star Circus School since 2012.

Vita is a certified instructor in the Progressive Ballet Technique curriculum. She has completed her background check and been Safety Certified through USA Gymnastics. Vita is committed to continuously researching, learning, and continuing her education through workshops, online courses, and personal research.

Vita is married to Bill Bachman, a world-renowned percussion instructor, and they have three young children.

For students of all levels

Methods

Vita's method combines carefully selected exercises for proper warm-up, flexibility, and strength development. Vita believes in a ballet-based technique enriched with rhythmic gymnastics training, proper body alignment and control. She encourages her students to expand their limits though consistent, gradual, and individual approach. Vita often advises, "Flexibility without strength can be dangerous." Students' safety and the longevity of their careers is most important, therefore Vita encourages each student to develop awareness of their internal boundaries and communicate them to the instructor.

In the day and age of easy access to various kinds of ideas, articles, training videos and classes it is easy to develop false perception of flexibility training. Many young artists aspire to imitate the more advanced ones and may not have enough knowledge to take into account factors which over time may lead to injuries:

  • using methods or intensity inappropriate for their age, skill level or physique
  • using wrong technique: improper body alignment and lack of muscle control
  • omitting strength and muscle memory development necessary to make their flexibility useful and safe
  • not balancing out their stretching on both sides evenly, but favoring their “good” side
  • pursuing fast results by stretching too forcefully

Vita works with students of all levels, from beginners to professional: dancers of various styles, rhythmic and artistic gymnasts, aerial dancers, contortionists, and figure skaters. She is aware of the nuances of each discipline and adjusts her lessons accordingly based on their activity, age, skill level, and personal goals.

Vita is passionate about giving her very young students a solid foundation to build upon which opens doors for various sports and activities. She offers developmental classes for 4-7 year olds which are based on rhythmic gymnastics training and emphasize overall physical development: muscle memory, proper body alignment, coordination, age-appropriate flexibility and strength training. Her youngest students usually transition really well into the discipline of their choice with a noticeable head start.
